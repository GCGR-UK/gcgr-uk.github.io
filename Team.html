Meet The Team

Steve Pollard obtained his PhD in zebrafish genetics from the MRC National Institute for Medical Research. He was a postdoc at the University of Edinburgh with Prof Austin Smith, where he first began studying mammalian neural stem cells. As a Wellcome Beit Fellow at the University of Cambridge he focused on neural stem cells and glioblastoma. He established his own research group at the UCL Cancer Institute, before moving in 2013 to the University of Edinburgh. Steve holds a CRUK Senior Fellowship, and in 2017 was awarded the Chair of Stem Cell and Cancer Biology. His laboratory continues to study the molecular and cellular mechanisms that regulate neural stem self-renewal and differentiation, and how these operate in the context of human brain tumours.

Steven Pollard

GCGR Academic Lead

Gillian Morrison obtained her PhD from the MRC Human Genetics Unit, Edinburgh. She studied developmental biology as a postdoc and was awarded a JDRF International Postdoctoral Fellowship to work on mammalian stem cell biology and cellular differentiation in the lab of Prof Austin Smith, University of Cambridge. In 2013 Gillian returned to Edinburgh as a Chancellors Fellow at the MRC Centre for Regenerative Medicine before joining the GCGR in 2017. As lead scientist and manager, Gillian brings with her over 20 years of experience in deriving, engineering and studying mammalian stem cells.

Gillian Morrison

Lead Scientist and Manager

Simona Parrinello is a group leader and professor of neuro-oncology at the UCL Cancer Institute, which she joined in 2018. Simona’s research focuses on adult neurogenesis and brain cancer. Her group studies the cellular and molecular mechanisms by which the microenvironment controls neural stem cell behavior and how these mechanisms become deregulated in glioblastoma. Prior to her recent move to UCL, Simona was a group leader and honorary Reader at the MRC London Institute of Medical Sciences, Imperial college London, where she first established her research group in 2011. She carried out her postdoctoral training at UCL as an EMBO and Dorothy Hodgkin Research Fellow in the group of Prof. Alison Lloyd and received her PhD from the University of California, at Berkeley under the supervision of Prof. Judith Campisi.

Simona Parrinello

UCL GCGR Academic Lead

Javier Herrero completed in PhD at the Spanish National Cancer Centre (CNIO) in 2004. Following this, he joined the Ensembl team at the European Bioinformatics Institute where he was both leading the Ensembl Comparative Genomics team and one of the coordinators of the Ensembl Project. Javier contributed to several large-scale genomics projects including ENCODE, Mouse ENCODE, the 1000 Genomes Project as well as several vertebrate genomes sequencing projects, from the lamprey to several primates. Since 2014, Javier is the Head of the Bill Lyons Informatics Centre, the the hub for computational biology and bioinformatics at the UCL Cancer Institute, University College London. His research involves the high-level interpretation of cancer genomics data focussing on the integration of different data sources for understanding the global changes and evolution of the cancer in patients.

Javier Herrero

Stephan Beck is Professor of Medical Genomics at UCL since 2007 and Director of the UK Personal Genome Project. He received his PhD in 1985 from Konstanz University where he studied DNA structure. After appointments at the MRC-LMB in Cambridge, Millipore Corporation in Boston, and the ICRF in London, he joined the Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute in 1996. During his tenure as Head of Human Sequencing, he played a leading role in the sequencing and analysis of the human genome. His current research focuses on the genomics and epigenomics of phenotypic plasticity in health and disease to advance translational, regenerative and personalized medicine.

Stephan Beck

Sebastian Brandner is a Professor of Neuropathy at UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology. He has research interests in neurodegeneration and neuro-oncology.

Sebastian Brandner

Paul Brennan is a Senior Clinical Lecturer and Honorary Consultant Neurosurgeon at the University of Edinburgh and NHS Lothian. He combines an active neurosurgical practice with clinical and laboratory research. He uses data science to understand relevant clinical problems, the solutions to which he develops through a combination of discovery science and clinical trials. He is part of the CRUK Adult Brain Tumour Centre of Excellence, based on Edinburgh. His research is contributing to speeding up diagnosis of brain tumours using symptom-based referral pathways and novel blood tests, understanding the cellular and molecular biology of high grade glioma recurrence, and developing novel strategies for delivering chemotherapy to brain tumours.

Paul Brennan
